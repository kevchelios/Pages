Grain

The 4th and final texture is the Grain background. Full transparency, I definitely needed a layup for this last one. Mainly cause I'd overcompensated on the previous textures and the steps to produce them. I needed something that was gonna be aesthetically still pleasing, but much, much more simple to produce.

Let's get started

  • We'll begin with creating a canvas that measures 1000 x 1000 pixels. This will allow us to create more density at better quality.
  • Let's cover the entire canvas with a rectangle and fill the color with the following hexcode.

    e7ce12

  • Next up is actually creating the texture. Create a new layer and head up to the menu selecting:

    Edit > Fill > Contents > Select 50% Gray

  • Let's convert the gray layer to a smart object
  • Head up to the filters menu and select:

    Filter > Noise > Add Noise

    I set my amount to about 9% under gaussian and monocrhomatic
  • Now that we have the noise, let's change the blend mode to color burn
  • Our final step is exporting that puppy and dropping it into the img folder because it's CSS ready.
